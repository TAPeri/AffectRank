Emotion annotation experiment

Institute of Digital Games

The purpose of this study is to evaluate the differences between two methods for emotion annotation.

Both methods require the annotator to quantify the emotional state of a person across two dimensions: arousal and valence.

  • Arousal: refers to the intensity of the emotions the person experiences.
    • High arousal: active, energetic, excited, etc.
    • Low arousal: inactive, quiet, peaceful, drowsy, etc.
  • Valence: refers to pleasantness or unpelasantness of the emotions the person experiences.
    • Positive valence: happy, excited, pleased, relaxed, etc.
    • Negative valence: sad, depressed, etc.

If you decide to participate in this study, you will have to annotate 5 videos using two alternative interfaces. Each of the videos last between 3 and 5 minutes and show a person either interacting with a virtual agent of playing a game.

The experiment can be paused and resumed later by introducing the same username.
